How we build

Process is not a story we tell.

It shows in the object. Every decision — material, method, time — is visible if you know where to look.

Step 01 / Materials

We begin at the
source, not the catalogue.

Every material we use has a name and a face behind it. A tannery in Somerset that has been operating since 1862. A brass foundry in northern Italy run by one family for three generations.

We visit before we order. We order less than we could use. We return if anything changes.

All leather sourced from LWG Gold-rated tanneries
Hardware cast from recycled brass, no plating
Technical fabrics woven to our own specification
Step 02 / Construction

Methods chosen for the object,
not the schedule.

We do not outsource quality control because we cannot — our makers are our designers in the truest sense. The hand that holds the needle decides what the object becomes.

Every product is finished and inspected by the maker before it leaves the workshop. Not by a separate QC team. By the person who built it.

Hand-stitched with waxed linen thread, 7 stitches per cm
Edges burnished by hand, no edge paint
Each piece signed by its maker on the interior
Step 03 / Time

Deadlines exist for
shipping. Not for craft.

We release when a thing is ready. That has meant delaying products by six months, scrapping entire runs, and starting over. We consider this completely normal.

A sneaker last takes 18 months to develop. We spent that time. The first run sold out in four hours. We did not rush the second run.

Average development cycle: 14–22 months per product
Leather aged minimum 90 days before cutting
No fast-follow products. Ever.
What we use

Materials with
provenance.

Vegetable-tanned Leather
Horween Leather Co. · Chicago, IL
Tanned using traditional oak bark methods. Takes 6 weeks minimum. Develops a patina unique to each owner. Used in bags, wallets, and shoe uppers.
Waxed Cotton Canvas
Halley Stevensons · Dundee, Scotland
Woven and waxed on-site since 1864. Waterproof without a membrane. Re-waxable indefinitely. Used in field jackets and backpacks.
Solid Brass Hardware
Riri Group · Mendrisio, Switzerland
Cast from recycled brass. Never plated — the surface you see is the material itself. Develops a warm patina with age. Used on all bags and straps.
Merino Wool Shell
Vitale Barberis Canonico · Biella, Italy
Woven to our 380g/m² specification. Naturally temperature-regulating. Used in the Wool Overcoat and select interior linings.
Technical Mesh
Schoeller Textil · Sevelen, Switzerland
A proprietary 3D-knit developed in collaboration. Lightweight, breathable, and extraordinarily durable. Used in sneaker uppers and technical linings.
Natural Rubber Outsoles
Vibram · Albizzate, Italy
Poured in small batches to our hardness specification. Designed to be resoled — the entire outsole unit is replaceable as a single piece.

We make things
you can repair.

Every AETHER product comes with a repair commitment. Not a warranty. A commitment — we will fix it, resole it, restitch it for as long as you own it.

This is not generosity. It is how we designed the objects. Every stitch is accessible. Every piece of hardware is replaceable. Every outsole is resoleable. We built it this way on purpose.

Send anything back. We will make it right.
